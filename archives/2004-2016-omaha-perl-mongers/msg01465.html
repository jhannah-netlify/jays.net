On Jul 31, 2007, at 2:17 PM, Jay Hannah wrote:


Substitution loop at CommonShare.pm line 65.

line 65 looks harmless enough:

  $d1=~s/[^\d]//g;

Anyone seen that error before?

Diving....  :)
Sure. The /g is a modifier saying "do this on everything throughout the string". In this case, it's removing every non-digit character from the string. 

It would be even better to have it as

   $dl =~ s/[^\d]+//g;
That way if you have a string of ten non-digits, they'll all get removed at once, rather via ten checks. 

You can also use the tr/// operator for this sort of thing, if you want.
